Definition 1.1. A collection of $A$ of subsets of a set $X$ is an $\mathbf{\text{algebra of sets}}$
if it satisfies the following axioms:

  • (a) The whole set $X$ and the empty set $\emptyset$ belong to $A$.
  • (b) The complement $X\setminus E$ of a set $E$ in $A$ belongs to $A$. (closed under complements)
  • (c) The union of a finite collection of sets in $A$ belongs to $A$.

Intuition: Groups in a Military Base

Abstraction is scary, so let’s try and build some intuition of what definition 1.1. means, by creating a non-abstract example.

As we can see, all the axioms of definition 1.1. are met, and we are therefore allowed to denote our collection $A$ an algebra of sets.

Extending $A$ into a Sigma-Algebra or Borel Field

Having covered the baseline, we are now in position to extend these concepts into a more powerful structure: the sigma-algebra, adding one additiona superpower: we are now allowed to take countably infinite unions of sets in the collection.

So basically, instead of a set $X$ of fixed size, we can now handle sets that can change in size dynamically: $$X = \{S1, S2, S3, S4, S5, \dots \}$$

Next, we add some kind of measure, for example, let’s say we want to tag (measure) which soliders that are part of our military base, that are currently either:

  • Fighting in the Northern sector, $A_N$
  • Fighting in the Western sector, $A_W$
  • Stationed on base, $A_B$

So the sigma-algebra $A$ represents all the soldiers/officers of our military base, that are “measurable” - meaning, wholse behaviour we can track/logg/reason about.

What new is that now we can keep tagging (updating) as things change. A unit might go from fighting in the Northern sector, to returning to the military base, $A_N \rightarrow A_B$. Our sigma-algebra ensures that even after thousands of hours of logging, no soldier will be logged in two states simultaneously, like S1 is both fighting in the Northern sector, $A_N$, while also chillin’ back at the military base, $A_B$. $$A_N \cup A_W \cup A_B \cdots$$

So the point is that our algebra of sets can only handle finite unions of sets, while our sigma-algebra can handle growing and even infinite sets. Which is useful for ongoing events, like $S1$ moving from fighting in the northern sector and returning back to base. We can also use them to hanble continous time, rather than discreet time.

Entering Measuable Space

By a measurable space we mean a pair $(X,\mathcal{X})$ consisting of an arbitrary set $X$ and a $\sigma \mathit{-algebra} \;\mathcal{X}$ of subsets of $X$. Sets in $\mathcal{X}$ are called measurable sets (with respect to the $\sigma \mathit{-algebra} \; \mathcal{X}$) or $\mathcal{X} \mathit{\text{-measurable sets}}$.
