Linear Algebra

An algebra consists of objects and rules for manipulating them. In Linear Algebra, the objects are vectors and the rules cover the manipulation of their position, and the addition and multiplication with each other.

The addition and scalar multiplication of vectors produce new vectors. The set of all vectors that can be created with the rules is called a vector set. A major idea is that of closure: will the addition or scalar multiplication of two vectors create a vector that is part of the original set?
