Location: Off World Situation: Three combat robots, each of different nationality, are facing off.

Theorem 1

Let players $P_1$, $P_2$, and $P_3$ fire with probabilities x, y, and z respectively, with x > y > z. The best strategy for all players is: 1 aims at 2, 2 aims at 1, and 3 aims at 1.

Rules of Engagement

Sequential Truel

  1. Combatants fire in sequence: $P_1$ fires first, then $P_2$, and then $P_3$.
  2. The probability that any $P$ hits her target is fixed: for $P_1$, the chance of
  3. All combatants are aware of others combatants abilities (probability of hitting a target)
  4. There is no abstaining (missing on purpose, firing into the air, not firing)
  5. Each combatant has an unlimited supply of ammunition
  6. A player can only pick one strategy for the entire truel.
  7. If a combatant is eliminated, then the remaining two combatants engage in a duel, until a single combantant remain.

Strategy

Once combatants are in position, they need to determine if there exists a best strategy, and if so what that strategy is.

At the start of a truel, each combatant has two targets to select from. This gives a eight possible strategies. There’s an assumption that each combatant is picking the strategy that is belived to maximise its chances of survial.

Theorem 1 Destroy the Stronger Opponent Strategy

Basically, in this strategy, each combatant targets the opponent with the greatest probability of hitting its target.

$$ \mathsf{\text{Let combatants }}\mathsf{C_1, C_2, C_3} \mathsf{\text{ fire with probabilities }} p, q, r \mathsf{\text{ respectively, where }} p>q>r. \\ \mathsf{\text{The best strategy for all combatants is that: }} \\ C_1 \mathsf{\text{ fires on }} C_2 \\ C_2 \mathsf{\text{ fires on }} C_1 \\ C_3 \mathsf{\text{ fires on }} C_1 \\ $$
